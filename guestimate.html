Guesstimates: More Than Just a Wild Guess

A Deep Dive into Estimation and Market Sizing

This resource provides a real-life consulting case study and a sample guesstimate to help you understand the core concepts. This is a typical “conceptual” case where problem identification and idea generation are tested, not just number crunching.

Consulting Case: Whisky Manufacturer

Background

The client was a first mover in promoting imported whisky in bars, discos and karaoke clubs in an emerging market about 10 years ago. They experienced tremendous success with over 30% annual growth by partnering with key outlets. However, growth has slowed to 10% per year, and profit margins are declining. The task is to understand the reasons for this slowdown and explore strategic options to rebuild growth.

Solving the Case

First question: What information would you require to help explain the slowing down of growth?

The Good Answer: One needs to understand what has been driving the slowdown. This involves considering the market and competitive environment for whisky.

  • The growth of the bar/disco channel is peaking.
  • The category has reached a high share of throat in the channel and is unlikely to grow by increasing penetration.
  • Competitors saw the opportunity and started to invest, eroding our client's market share.

The Excellent Answer: All of the above, but also considering the life-cycle and upgrade path for consumers as well as the aging of the brand image.

  • The client has used the same advertising campaign for 5 years.
  • An aging brand image impairs the ability to recruit new consumers.
  • There is a lack of aspiration for consumers to up-trade to more premium SKUs.
  • The category life cycle is short as consumers tend to go less to bars/discos as they age.

Second question: What is driving profitability down?

The Good Answer: One needs to understand the market forces affecting the bottom line of luxury consumer goods.

  • Competition has driven prices down and limited margin growth.
  • Trade marketing costs have increased as outlet owners have more bargaining power.
  • Imported goods are subject to currency fluctuations.

The Excellent Answer: All of the above, but also considering the profit-per-outlet aspect.

  • As the business expanded, partnering with smaller and weaker outlets (with lower ROI) drove down the overall return on investment.

Third question: What are the potential strategic options?

Here are some options that can be explored:

  • Revitalize the brand with a new campaign to improve competitiveness.
  • Premiumize the market and encourage consumers to up-trade to higher value products.
  • Seek opportunities in rural areas and invest strategically to capture less competitive markets.
  • Explore new channels such as in-home or restaurant consumption.
  • Innovate to improve on delivery and create a competitive advantage.
  • Make improvements in outlet selection criteria.

Sample Guesstimate

Question

A washing machine manufacturing company wants to enter the Indian Market. Estimate the number of washing machines they would sell, assuming their market share to be 2%?

Solution

This guesstimate should be approached from the demand side. The major users are households.

Population & Household Assumptions:

  • Total Population: 120 crores
  • Average family size: 4 members
  • Population Distribution: Cities (30%), Towns (25%), Villages (45%)

Penetration Assumptions:

  • Villages: 1 in 100 families have a washing machine.
  • Cities: Poor (20% pop, 1/50 families), Medium (45% pop, 4/5 families), Rich (35% pop, 1/1 families)
  • Towns: Poor (40% pop, minimal), Medium (40% pop, 1/5 families), Rich (20% pop, 4/5 families)

Based on calculations from the above data, the total number of washing machines in India is estimated to be 8.37 crores.

Replacement Market Calculation:

  • Average age of a washing machine: 8 years
  • Annual replacement rate: 1/8
  • Company's market share: 2%
  • Machines sold to replacement market = 8.37 crores * (1/8) * (2/100) = 2.09 lakhs

New Customer Market Calculation:

  • Average market growth rate: 10%
  • New customers = 10% of 8.37 crores = 0.837 crores
  • Company's market share: 2%
  • Machines sold to new customers = 0.837 crores * (2/100) = 1.67 lakhs

Total machines sold annually = 2.09 lakhs + 1.67 lakhs = 3.76 lakhs.
